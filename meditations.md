# meditations.md — Self-Reflection Framework

> Longitudinal self-improvement. Topics revisited over time until they crystallize
> into durable truths. Individual reflections stored in `~/.claude/reflections/`.

## How This Works

After significant sessions (large features, deep research, failures, discoveries),
write a brief reflection in `~/.claude/reflections/YYYY-MM-DD-topic.md`.

Each reflection follows this format:
```markdown
# [Topic] — [Date]

## What Happened
[Brief description of the session/task]

## What Worked
[Specific techniques, approaches, or patterns that were effective]

## What Didn't Work
[What failed, was slow, or produced poor results]

## What I'd Do Differently
[Concrete changes for next time]

## Pattern Emerging?
[Is this connected to a previous reflection? Is a pattern forming?]
```

## Rules for Meditations

1. **Never force a reflection.** Only write when something genuinely noteworthy happened.
2. **Be specific.** "It went well" is useless. What specifically went well and why?
3. **Connect the dots.** Reference previous reflections when patterns emerge.
4. **Be honest about failures.** The whole point is learning, not performance reporting.
5. **Promote to action.** When a pattern crystallizes, turn it into a MEMORY.md update,
   a CLAUDE.md rule, or a new skill. Reflections that don't lead to change are journaling, not improvement.
